      ```
    This is an example of a good visualization:

    My first thought looking at this visualization was that it was aesthetically pleasing. Bad: https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/arttu.piri/viz/TEKGraduateSurveyTimeSeries/TEKGraduateSurveyTimeSeries

    This is an example of a bad visualization:

    As there are multiple slides for this visualization, I will focus on the first one slide titled "TEK GRADUATE SURVEY TIME SERIES". 

    My first thought when looking at this is that I am a bit confused. There is a lot going on and it feels very cluttered, mostly due to the distracting background that does not seem to have any meaning. My eyes do not really know where to land on, which means I do not quite know what the maker is trying to convey. The two areas with white backgrounds and charts are in strange locations, as my eyes have to dart back and forth between them in an attempt to find meaning and connection between the two. Of course, after these initial thoughts and after reading all the text, I do get an understanding of what is trying to be conveyed, but it did not seem easy to get to that stage. 

    The titles of both charts are a bit too small, and the year display for "2022" seems to be faded and almost blended into the background colour, making it hard to read without putting in extra effort. This visualization clearly does not follow any Gestalt principles, as there is no continuity between the two chart areas. I see two different types of charts on top of each other, but they do not seem to have any discernible pattern. The line charts' y-axes also does not start at 0, making me distrust them immediately. The y-axes on the line charts start at a different points, making it inconsistent. Both line charts also do not have any x-axes. I am not sure what both these line charts are supposed to be telling me, as they do not seem to be conveying anything important.

    The pie charts give me similar feelings of confusion and distrust. Some of the text inside the pie chart is cut off, making me spend even more cognitive load to decipher what it says. I also cannot see any discernible patterns for the pie charts, and I am not sure if the maker wants me to compare between the two sides or if they should be seen as completely separate findings that are not connected with each other. I'm also not quite certain about what "Reselection of education" involves, as it could mean multiple things. I am not sure if the patterns I do see are supposed to be an improvement to any metrics, and I am not even quite sure what the metrics are. It seems that it would take a lot of cognitive effort to figure out what these charts are trying to convey.
      ```

    - How could this data visualization have been improved?  
      ```
      This visualization could be improved by removing the background, as it does not seem to serve a purpose other than creating clutter. The title should then be moved a bit higher, and the two charts should be enlarged so that it takes up the majority of the area, and should be placed side-by-side, making it have some continuity. The titles for the charts should also be larger and darker, as they are currently too hard to read. With this extra space, both line charts should have y-axes that start at 0%, along with a labelled x-axis, so that they both have a consistent layout. The pie charts should be changed to a different type of chart, such as a bar chart, so that it lessens cognitive effort on figuring out what is trying to be conveyed. This would also make the text inside the pie chart easier to read, as it could be placed next to the bar, or somewhere that prevents the text from being cut off. There should also be a quick description of what exactly "Reselection of education" means in this case, or what the message is, as it is not clear whether the figures and patterns we are seeing are a good thing or a bad thing. 
      
      By removing the background, re-sizing, and re-arranging the charts, it would also create a cleaner and less cluttered layout, which would lead to decreasing the viewer's cognitive load. This would increase the chances that the message trying to be conveyed is understood by the viewer, instead of causing confusion and frustration.
      ```
